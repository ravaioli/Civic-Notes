# Creating a Guild

Guilds are the heart of collective action on CivicNotes. They transform individual voices into organized power, enabling neighbors with shared concerns to coordinate, amplify each other's impact, and participate in platform governance. This guide walks you through the entire process of forming and verifying a guild—from initial idea to active collective.

---

## What Is a Guild?

A **guild** is a verified, self‑organizing collective of CivicNotes users who share a common civic purpose. Guilds are more than just interest groups—they are formal entities with defined governance, collective decision‑making, and special privileges on the platform.

### What Guilds Can Do

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Endorse notes** | Guild‑endorsed notes receive a badge and increased visibility. |
| **Sponsor proposals** | Create and submit formal proposals from the Regenerative Action Library. |
| **Launch petitions** | Guild‑sponsored petitions carry extra weight and appear in guild feeds. |
| **Verify promises** | Guild verifications count more than individual ones in Promise Tracker votes. |
| **Participate in governance** | Guild representatives form the Guild Council, guiding platform decisions. |
| **Access analytics** | See collective impact metrics and member engagement data. |
| **Communicate internally** | Private discussion spaces and announcement tools. |

### What Guilds Must Do

| Responsibility | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Maintain a charter** | Public document stating purpose, governance, and membership criteria. |
| **Ensure representation** | Membership should reflect the community they claim to represent. |
| **Participate in governance** | Engage in Guild Council activities and platform‑wide decisions. |
| **Follow Code of Conduct** | All members must adhere to community standards. |
| **Regular re‑verification** | Update charter and confirm activity every two years. |

## Before You Start

### Prerequisites

To create a guild, you need:

- **At least 3 founding members** who are verified CivicNotes users (accounts older than 30 days, with at least 5 CivicNotes each recommended).
- **A clear purpose** – What issue or area will your guild focus on? (e.g., pedestrian safety, affordable housing, school gardens)
- **A proposed governance structure** – How will your guild make decisions? (consensus, majority vote, elected officers, rotating leadership)
- **A charter draft** – A public document outlining your guild's mission, values, and operating principles.

### Is a Guild Right for Your Group?

Guilds are ideal for groups that:
- Have an ongoing, long‑term focus (not a single‑issue campaign)
- Want to build collective power and visibility
- Are willing to participate in platform governance
- Can maintain active engagement over time

If your group is focused on a single petition or short‑term campaign, consider using [Petitions](../user-guide/petitions.md) instead. You can always form a guild later if the campaign evolves into ongoing work.

## Step 1: Draft Your Charter

Your guild's charter is a public document that explains who you are, what you stand for, and how you operate. It's required for verification and helps potential members understand your guild.

### Charter Template
```markdown
[Guild Name] Charter
Mission
[1–2 sentences describing your guild's purpose]

Focus Areas
[Primary focus, e.g., pedestrian safety]

[Secondary focus, e.g., bike infrastructure]

[Additional focus, e.g., transit equity]

Membership
Who can join? (e.g., open to all District 3 residents, or by application)

How are new members approved?

Are there membership expectations (e.g., attend meetings, vote on endorsements)?

Governance
How are decisions made? (e.g., consensus, simple majority, supermajority)

Leadership structure (e.g., rotating facilitators, elected stewards, no formal leaders)

Meeting frequency (e.g., monthly, quarterly, as needed)

Conflict Resolution
How will internal disagreements be handled?

Escalation path if conflicts cannot be resolved internally

Commitment to Platform
Willingness to participate in Guild Council

Agreement to follow CivicNotes Code of Conduct

Amendment Process
How can this charter be changed?

Last updated: [Date]
```

### Tips for a Strong Charter

- **Be specific** – Vague missions make it hard for potential members to know if they belong.
- **Keep governance simple** – Start with lightweight processes; you can always adapt.
- **Include diversity considerations** – How will you ensure your guild represents the community?
- **Make it public‑friendly** – This document will be visible to everyone on the platform.

## Step 2: Gather Founding Members

You need at least 3 verified CivicNotes users to apply. Before proceeding:

- Confirm each founding member's account is in good standing (no active suspensions).
- Discuss and agree on the charter together.
- Decide on initial leadership or facilitation roles.
- Collect member consent to be listed as founders.

Founding members will be publicly listed on the guild's profile page.

## Step 4: Community Comment Period

Once submitted, your application enters a **14‑day community comment period**. During this time:

- Your draft charter and application are publicly visible.
- Any CivicNotes user can leave comments, ask questions, or raise concerns.
- You may receive notifications about comments and can respond publicly.

### During This Period

- **Monitor comments** – Check regularly and respond thoughtfully to questions.
- **Clarify if needed** – If someone misunderstands your purpose, provide explanation.
- **Do not argue** – Stay constructive; defensiveness may harm your application.
- **Gather support** – Encourage founding members and allies to leave supportive comments.

The comment period ensures transparency and allows the community to flag potential issues before verification.

---

## Step 5: Verification Vote

After the comment period closes, your application moves to a **verification vote**. The voting body consists of:

- **All currently verified guilds** (each guild gets 1 vote)
- **Individual users with high Civic Reputation** (top 5% by Civic Seeds, votes weighted by reputation)

### Vote Threshold

To pass, your application needs **60% of weighted votes cast** in favor. Abstentions do not count.

### What Happens During Voting

- You'll receive a notification when voting opens.
- Voting typically lasts 7 days.
- You cannot vote for your own guild, but you can encourage supporters to participate.
- Results are announced publicly.

### Possible Outcomes

| Outcome | What Happens Next |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Approved** | Congratulations! Your guild is now verified. You'll receive setup instructions. |
| **Not Approved** | You'll receive feedback on why the vote failed. You can revise and reapply after 30 days. |
| **Conditional Approval** | Rare; may require charter changes before final approval. |

---

## Step 6: Post‑Verification Setup

Once approved, you'll gain access to your guild dashboard and can begin setting up.

### Immediate Tasks

1. **Customize your guild profile**
   - Upload a profile image/logo
   - Set a cover image
   - Review and publish your charter
   - Add contact information (public‑facing email or link)

2. **Configure membership settings**
   - Open membership (anyone can join)
   - Approval required (admin must approve each request)
   - Invite‑only (new members must be invited)

3. **Set up internal communication**
   - Guild announcement channel
   - Discussion forum (threaded)
   - Decide on communication tools (in‑platform or external like Signal/Discord)

4. **Establish initial leadership**
   - Assign guild admins (can manage members, edit profile)
   - Define roles if needed (e.g., facilitator, secretary)

5. **Create your first proposal or endorsement**
   - Start building momentum by endorsing relevant CivicNotes or creating a proposal

## Guild Roles and Permissions

| Role | Can Do | Typically |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Member** | Participate in discussions, vote on internal decisions, suggest endorsements | All guild members |
| **Endorsement Manager** | Review and approve endorsement suggestions, cast guild's endorsement vote | Selected active members |
| **Admin** | Manage members, edit guild profile, create announcements, represent guild in Guild Council | Founding members or elected leads |
| **Founder** | Special badge; can transfer ownership, delete guild (rare) | Original applicants |

Roles can be assigned from the **Members** tab in your guild dashboard.

## Guild Responsibilities

### Ongoing Requirements

| Requirement | Frequency | Description |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Active participation** | Ongoing | Guilds that are inactive for 12 months may be archived. |
| **Guild Council participation** | Quarterly | Send a representative to Guild Council meetings. |
| **Charter review** | Every 2 years | Update charter if needed and confirm continued alignment. |
| **Conflict of Interest disclosure** | Annual | Update any changes in funding or affiliations. |

### Re‑Verification

Every two years, guilds must undergo a **light re‑verification**:

1. Submit a brief update on activities, membership changes, and any charter updates.
2. The update is published for a 30‑day community comment period.
3. If no significant objections are raised, status continues.
4. If objections arise, a full verification vote may be triggered.

This ensures guilds remain accountable to the community they represent.

## Common Questions

### How many people do we need to start?
Minimum 3 verified founding members. More is better for credibility and momentum.

### Can we be a guild if we're an existing organization?
Yes! Existing nonprofits, neighborhood associations, and advocacy groups are welcome. You'll need to create charters that align with CivicNotes principles.

### Can we have paid staff?
Guilds themselves are non‑commercial entities on CivicNotes. Individual members may have paid roles in outside organizations, but guild activities on the platform should be volunteer‑driven. Disclose any funding sources in your application.

### What if we disagree internally?
Your charter should include a conflict resolution process. If disputes cannot be resolved, guilds can request mediation from the Moderation Council.

### Can we leave the guild?
Members can leave at any time. If a founder leaves, the guild should update its founding member list (though the guild remains verified).

### Can a guild be dissolved?
Yes. A guild can vote to dissolve itself. Remaining assets (if any, e.g., unspent bounty funds) would be transferred to the Guild Council for community use.

### Can we be in multiple guilds?
Absolutely. Many users belong to several guilds reflecting different interests.

## Tips for Success

### First 30 Days
- **Endorse 3–5 existing notes** – Show your guild is active and cares about specific issues.
- **Create one proposal** – Use the Regenerative Action Library to draft something concrete.
- **Recruit 5–10 members** – Reach out to neighbors who have left relevant notes.
- **Attend Guild Council orientation** – Learn how platform governance works.

### Building Momentum
- **Celebrate wins** – When a promise you endorsed is addressed, share it!
- **Collaborate with other guilds** – Co‑sponsor petitions or proposals.
- **Host events** – Use the platform to organize meetups or listening sessions.
- **Document impact** – Track your guild's Φ‑contribution and share with members.

### Avoiding Common Pitfalls
- **Don't try to do everything** – Focus on your stated mission.
- **Keep governance lightweight** – Start simple; add structure as needed.
- **Communicate regularly** – Silence leads to disengagement.
- **Be transparent** – Your charter and decisions are public for a reason.
