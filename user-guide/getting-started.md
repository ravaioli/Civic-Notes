# Getting Started with CivicNotes

Welcome to CivicNotes! This guide will walk you through your first steps on the platform, from creating an account to leaving your first CivicNote. By the end, you'll be ready to participate in your local civic conversations and start making your voice heard.

---

## What You'll Need

- An email address (or a social/login method we support)
- Your home address or neighborhood (for verification – we never share this publicly)
- About 10 minutes to complete the setup

---

## Step 1: Create Your Account

1. Visit [app.civicnotes.org](https://app.civicnotes.org) (or your local instance).
2. Click **"Sign Up"** in the top right corner.
3. Choose your sign-up method:
   - Email and password
   - Google / GitHub / other OAuth provider
4. Accept the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) and [Privacy Policy](../privacy.md).

*Your email is never shared publicly. We use it only for account recovery and important notifications (which you can control).*

---

## Step 2: Verify Your Neighborhood

To help you connect with neighbours and see locally relevant content, we ask for your general location.

1. Enter your street address or neighbourhood name.
2. Confirm on the map that your location is correct.
3. Choose your verification method:
   - **SMS code** (fastest – we send a one‑time code to your phone)
   - **Postal mail** (we send a postcard with a code – takes a few days)
   - **In‑person at a community event** (check our [Events](../community/events.md) page)

> 🔒 **Privacy Promise:** Your exact address is never shown publicly. Only your neighbourhood or district appears on your profile. We use precise location only to filter content and verify that you're a real resident.

Once verified, you'll see "Verified Resident" on your profile.

## Step 4: Find a Meeting or Agenda Item

Let's find something to engage with.

1. Click **"Agenda"** in the top navigation bar.
2. You'll see a list of upcoming meetings, sorted by date.
3. Click on **"City Council – April 15, 2025"** (or whatever is next).
4. Browse the agenda items. Each one shows:
   - A **Φ‑Score pill** (e.g., a green "78")
   - A **sentiment summary** (support/oppose/question/proposal)
   - How many notes have been left
5. Click on an item that interests you – for example, **"Item #7: Zoning Amendment – 5th and Main"**.

You're now on the agenda item page. Here you can read the official description, see what your neighbours are saying, and leave your own note.

---

## Step 5: Leave Your First CivicNote

Ready to add your voice?

1. On the agenda item page, scroll to the **"Leave a CivicNote"** box.
2. Type your comment. For example:
   > "This intersection is already dangerous for kids walking to school. Rezoning without a traffic calming plan is reckless."
3. Choose an **Intent Tag** that best matches your note:
   - 🔵 **Support** – You agree with the proposal.
   - 🔴 **Oppose** – You disagree.
   - 🟡 **Question** – You need more information.
   - 🟢 **Proposal** – You have an alternative idea.
   - 👁️ **Witness** – You're noting something about the process (not taking a stance).
4. Click **"Post Note"**.

That's it! Your note is now public and visible to your neighbours, local guilds, and even officials. The agenda item's Φ‑Score will update to reflect your participation.

---

## Step 6: Explore What's Next

Now that you've left your first note, here are some natural next steps:

### 🔍 Discover Guilds
- Go to the **"Guilds"** tab.
- Browse guilds active in your area (e.g., "Safe Streets Guild," "Housing Justice").
- Click **"Join"** on any that match your interests. (Some guilds require approval.)

### 📢 Find Petitions to Sign
- Visit the **"Petitions"** section.
- Filter by your district or topics you care about.
- Click **"Sign"** on any petition you support – you'll need to verify your signature (SMS or email).

### 👁️ Watch a Meeting
- From any agenda item, click **"Watch Live"** or **"Watch Recording"** (if available).
- While watching, keep an ear out for officials mentioning notes like yours – that's your cue to propose a Promise later.

### 🔗 Connect Your Profile (Optional)
- Add a profile picture.
- Write a short bio (e.g., "Parent, gardener, concerned neighbour").
- Link to a personal website or social media (optional).

## Understanding Your Impact

As you participate, you'll notice your personal metrics grow:

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|------------------|
| **Civic Seeds** | Reputation points earned by leaving notes, verifying promises, and contributing constructively. |
| **Notes Left** | Total number of CivicNotes you've posted. |
| **Promises Verified** | How many promises you've helped validate. |
| **Promises Fulfilled** | How many promises you've proposed that were ultimately addressed. |
| **Guild Memberships** | Which guilds you belong to. |

Your profile page shows these metrics publicly (unless you choose to hide them), building a record of your civic engagement over time.

---

## Tips for Meaningful Participation

- **Be specific:** Notes with clear, local details are more likely to be acknowledged.
- **Use the right intent tag:** This helps others understand your perspective at a glance.
- **Verify others' promises:** It only takes a moment and strengthens the whole community's accountability.
- **Join a guild:** Collective action amplifies individual voices.
- **Celebrate successes:** When a promise is addressed, share it – positive reinforcement matters.
