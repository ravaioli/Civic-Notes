# Core Concepts

This guide introduces the fundamental ideas and building blocks of CivicNotes. Understanding these concepts will help you navigate the platform, participate meaningfully, and see how your engagement contributes to the health of your local democracy.

---

## The Φ‑Score (Phi Score)

The Φ‑Score is a composite metric (0–100) that measures the civic health of an agenda item, a neighborhood, a guild, or even the entire city. It makes the state of local democracy legible at a glance.

### What the Φ‑Score Measures

The Φ‑Score combines four weighted sub‑metrics:

| Sub‑metric | Weight | What It Measures |
|------------|--------|------------------|
| **Participation** | 30% | Volume of CivicNotes relative to population. Are people showing up? |
| **Clarity** | 25% | Coherence of sentiment. Is there clear consensus or well‑articulated diverse views, or is it noise and trolling? |
| **Responsiveness** | 30% | How often officials acknowledge or act on citizen input (tracked via the Promise Tracker). |
| **Connections** | 15% | Density of relationships – replies, guild endorsements, cross‑neighborhood engagement. |

### Reading the Φ‑Score

The Φ‑Score is always displayed with a color and often a subtle animation:

| Range | Color | Meaning | Animation |
|-------|-------|---------|-----------|
| **71–100** | Vibrant cyan to seafoam green | Thriving – healthy, engaged discourse | Slow, gentle pulse |
| **41–70** | Gold to deep amber | Stable or stressed – active but with friction | Faster shimmer |
| **0–40** | Burnt orange to deep crimson | Fragile or critical – low engagement or high conflict | Weak, erratic flicker |

You'll see Φ‑Scores on:
- Agenda items – to gauge community interest before a meeting
- Neighborhoods/districts – to compare civic health across the city
- Guild profiles – to see a guild's impact
- Your personal dashboard – to track trends over time

> **Tip:** Click any Φ‑Score to expand it and see the four sub‑metrics. This helps you understand *why* a score is what it is.

## CivicNotes

A **CivicNote** is the basic unit of conversation on the platform. It's a short public comment on a specific agenda item.

### Anatomy of a CivicNote
```markdown
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Alex · District 3 · Apr 12, 2025 · [Proposal 💡] |
| |
| This intersection is already dangerous for kids walking to |
| school. Rezoning without a traffic calming plan is reckless. |
| |
| [👍 12] [💬 3] [🔗 2] [Suggest Guild Endorsement] |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Intent Tags

Every CivicNote includes an **intent tag** – a crucial signal that helps aggregate sentiment and guides the Promise Tracker.

| Tag | Icon | When to Use |
|-----|------|-------------|
| **Support** | 🔵 | You agree with the proposal or idea. |
| **Oppose** | 🔴 | You disagree. |
| **Question** | 🟡 | You need more information or clarification. |
| **Proposal** | 🟢 | You have an alternative idea or solution. |
| **Witness** | 👁️ | You're noting something about the process, not taking a stance (e.g., "The meeting started late," "This item was rushed"). |

Intent tags power the **sentiment bars** you see on agenda items, giving everyone an at‑a‑glance view of community opinion.

## Promise Tracker

The **Promise Tracker** is CivicNotes' accountability engine. It creates a transparent, community‑verified record of when officials acknowledge or act on citizen input.

### How Promises Work

1. **Propose** – After watching a meeting, you notice an official mention an idea from a CivicNote. You click "Propose Acknowledgment," paste the video timestamp or minutes excerpt, and link it to the original note.
2. **Verify** – The proposed promise enters a 7‑day verification period. Other community members review the evidence and vote on whether it's accurate.
3. **Confirmed** – If enough verifications are reached (e.g., 5 votes), the promise becomes **Verified**. It now appears in the official's dashboard.
4. **Respond** – The official can acknowledge the promise publicly (which updates status to **Acknowledged**) or eventually mark it as **Addressed** with evidence of action.
5. **Celebrate** – Addressed promises increase the Φ‑Score and award Civic Seeds to everyone who participated.

### Promise Statuses

| Status | Color | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Proposed** | Gray | Awaiting community verification. |
| **Verified** | Blue | Community‑confirmed as accurate. |
| **Acknowledged** | Purple | Official has publicly responded. |
| **Addressed** | Green | Action has been taken (with evidence). |
| **Disputed** | Red | Under review by moderators. |

The Promise Tracker transforms "they never listen" into a visible, auditable record of exactly who listened, when, and what happened next.

## Guilds

A **Guild** is a verified, self‑organizing collective of CivicNotes users who share a common civic purpose. Guilds amplify individual voices and enable coordinated action.

### Examples of Guilds

- **Safe Streets Guild** – Focused on pedestrian safety, crosswalks, traffic calming
- **Housing Justice Guild** – Advocating for affordable housing and tenant rights
- **School Food Sovereignty Guild** – Working toward healthier school meals
- **Watershed Guardians** – Monitoring local water quality and restoration

### What Guilds Can Do

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Endorse notes** | A guild badge appears on endorsed notes, boosting visibility. |
| **Sponsor proposals** | Guilds can create and submit formal proposals from the Regenerative Action Library. |
| **Launch petitions** | Guild‑sponsored petitions carry extra weight. |
| **Verify promises** | Guild verifications count more than individual ones. |
| **Participate in governance** | Guild representatives form the Guild Council, which helps guide platform decisions. |

### Joining a Guild

1. Visit the **Guilds** tab.
2. Browse by focus area or neighborhood.
3. Click **"Join"** on any guild that interests you. Some guilds require approval from existing members.
4. Once approved, you'll see guild‑specific tools in your dashboard.

You can belong to multiple guilds. Your profile will show your affiliations.

## Petitions

**Petitions** are formal expressions of community will, often derived from CivicNotes or Regenerative Actions. They collect verified signatures and create direct pressure for official response.

### Petition Lifecycle

1. **Creation** – Anyone can start a petition from a CivicNote, a proposal, or by using the Regenerative Action Library. You set a target (e.g., City Council), a signature goal, and a deadline.
2. **Signing** – Neighbors sign using verified methods (SMS, email, in‑person). Each signature adds to the count.
3. **Threshold Reached** – When the goal is met, the petition automatically creates a **Promise Tracker record** targeting the relevant body.
4. **Official Response** – The target body receives a notification. They can respond publicly, and their response is tracked.
5. **Outcome** – If the request is granted, the petition is marked **Addressed**. If not, it remains as a public record of community will.

### Signature Verification

To prevent fraud, signatures must be verified:
- **SMS** – Receive a code by text message
- **Email** – Click a confirmation link
- **In‑person** – Scan a QR code at community events

Verified signatures carry more weight in the Φ‑Score calculation.

## Regenerative Actions

**Regenerative Actions** are a library of pre‑defined, strategically aligned interventions drawn from the Solarpunk Mandala framework. They provide a starting point for turning concern into constructive proposals.

### Action Structure

Each Regenerative Action includes:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Title** | Short, descriptive name (e.g., "15‑Minute Community Power Commons") |
| **Domain** | Ecology, Economics, Politics, Spirituality, or Culture |
| **Topic** | More specific category (e.g., "Proximity and Access," "Air Quality") |
| **Pathway** | Liberation, Healing, or Awakening – the type of change it enables |
| **Liberate Action** | The proposed solution or intervention |
| **Oppress Action** | What it's pushing against – provides context |
| **Success Indicators** | Measurable outcomes if implemented |
| **Circulation Check** | Why this action is regenerative, not extractive |

### Using Regenerative Actions

1. Browse the **Action Library** from the main navigation.
2. Filter by domain, topic, or pathway.
3. Click an action to see full details.
4. Click **"Adapt for My Community"** to localize it – add your neighborhood, target body, and specific details.
5. Choose output: **Proposal** (for an agenda item) or **Petition** (to gather signatures).

Actions ensure that civic energy is channeled into well‑considered, regenerative solutions rather than reactive complaints.

## The Visible Heart

The **Visible Heart** is both a design philosophy and a literal feature of CivicNotes. It means:

- **Making the invisible visible** – Φ‑Scores, sentiment, promises, and relationships are all displayed openly.
- **Real‑time awareness** – As the community engages, the Φ‑Score updates, creating a living dashboard of civic health.
- **Collective sense‑making** – Instead of isolated opinions, users see patterns, trends, and the web of relationships.

You'll encounter the Visible Heart in:
- The **Φ‑Score heatmap** of your city
- The **Promise Tracker's** public ledger
- The **network visualizations** on guild profiles
- The **sentiment bars** on agenda items

## How the Concepts Connect
```markdown
CivicNotes ──┬── Φ‑Score (measures health of everything below)
│
├── CivicNotes (individual voices)
│
├── Promise Tracker (tracks official response)
│
├── Guilds (collective action)
│
├── Petitions (formal community will)
│
└── Regenerative Actions (solution templates)

All feed back into the Φ‑Score, creating a continuous loop:
Participation → Visibility → Accountability → Trust → More Participation
```

## From Old to New: A Paradigm Shift

| Old Way | CivicNotes Way |
|---------|----------------|
| Isolated comments disappear | CivicNotes are visible, searchable, and trackable |
| "They never listen" | Promise Tracker shows exactly who listened and when |
| Individual voices get lost | Guilds amplify collective power |
| Reactive complaints | Regenerative Actions offer constructive alternatives |
| Opacity | The Visible Heart makes everything legible |
| Civic health is a feeling | Φ‑Score makes it measurable |
