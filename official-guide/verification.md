# Official Verification

For government officials and staff to access their specialized dashboard and tools, they must first verify their identity on CivicNotes. This process ensures that only legitimate representatives of public bodies can claim official accounts, maintaining trust and preventing impersonation. This guide walks through the verification process step by step.

---

## Who Can Be Verified

The following roles are eligible for official verification:

| Role | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Elected Officials** | City Council members, School Board members, Mayors, Commissioners |
| **Government Staff** | City Clerks, Planning Department staff, Legislative Aides, Agency Directors |
| **Board/Commission Members** | Planning Commission, Parks Commission, Zoning Board of Appeals |
| **Public Agency Representatives** | Library Board, Port Authority, Transit District |

If you hold a public position and regularly participate in or support public meetings, you're likely eligible.

---

## Why Verify?

Verifying your official status gives you access to:

- **Official Dashboard** – A specialized view of upcoming meetings, constituent sentiment, and actionable items.
- **Promise Tracker Inbox** – See and respond to promises that mention you or your body.
- **Aggregated Analytics** – Understand district‑wide Φ‑scores, top concerns, and engagement trends.
- **Response Tools** – Acknowledge promises, comment on petitions, and communicate with constituents directly.
- **Public Badge** – Your profile displays an [🏛️ Official] badge, signaling your role to the community.
- **Staff Delegation** – Designate aides to help manage your official presence.

Verification also demonstrates your commitment to transparency and responsive governance.

## Verification Methods

CivicNotes offers multiple ways to verify your official status. Choose the method that works best for you.

### Method 1: Government Email Address (Fastest)

If you have a .gov or official government email address, this is the quickest method.

**What you'll need:**
- Your official government email (e.g., `@cityname.gov`, `@countyname.org`, `@school district.k12.state.us`)
- Access to that email account

**Process:**

1. From your CivicNotes profile, click **"Request Official Verification."**
2. Select **"Government Email"** as your method.
3. Enter your official email address.
4. A verification code will be sent to that email.
5. Enter the code to confirm.
6. Your account now displays **"Verification Pending"** while our team does a final check (usually 1–2 business days).

> **Note:** Some smaller municipalities may not have `.gov` domains. If your email ends in `.org` or another domain but is clearly official, you may still use this method – our team will review manually if needed.

### Method 2: Official Letter or Document

If you don't have a government email, you can verify using official documentation.

**What you'll need:**
- A digital copy (photo or scan) of official letterhead, business card, or government ID
- The document must clearly show:
  - Your full name
  - Your official title
  - The government body you represent
  - (Optional but helpful) A recent date

**Process:**

1. From your profile, click **"Request Official Verification."**
2. Select **"Official Document"** as your method.
3. Upload your document (PDF, JPG, or PNG).
4. Add any clarifying notes (e.g., "My agency doesn't issue email accounts").
5. Submit. Our team will review within 3–5 business days.
6. You'll receive notification when verification is complete or if more information is needed.

> **Privacy:** Documents are reviewed only for verification purposes and are deleted from our systems within 30 days.

### Method 3: In‑Person Verification at a Public Meeting

For officials who prefer face‑to‑face verification, or if other methods aren't feasible.

**What you'll need:**
- To attend a public meeting (City Council, School Board, etc.) where you are serving in your official capacity
- A government‑issued ID (optional, but helpful)

**Process:**

1. From your profile, click **"Request Official Verification."**
2. Select **"In‑Person"** as your method.
3. You'll see a list of upcoming meetings where CivicNotes has a verification partner (often the City Clerk's office).
4. Select a meeting and request an appointment time.
5. Attend the meeting and meet with the verification contact (e.g., the Clerk).
6. They will confirm your identity and role, then mark you as verified in the system.
7. You'll receive confirmation within 24 hours.

This method also helps build relationships with the staff who manage public records.

### Method 4: Reference from an Existing Verified Official

If you know another verified official (e.g., a colleague already on CivicNotes), they can vouch for you.

**What you'll need:**
- The CivicNotes username of a verified official from your same body or jurisdiction
- Their agreement to vouch for you

**Process:**

1. From your profile, click **"Request Official Verification."**
2. Select **"Reference from Verified Official."**
3. Enter the username of your reference.
4. They will receive a notification asking them to confirm your identity and role.
5. Once they confirm, your account is verified.

This method is useful for staff who may not appear in public meetings but support elected officials.

## The Verification Process: What Happens Behind the Scenes
```markdown
Request Submitted → Initial Review → (if needed) Additional Info → Approval → Badge Added
```

### Step 1: Request Submitted
You complete one of the verification methods above. Your request enters a queue.

### Step 2: Initial Review
A CivicNotes verification team member (or automated system for email) checks your information against public records where possible:
- Is the email domain associated with a government body?
- Does the name match elected/appointed officials listed on official websites?
- Does the document appear authentic?

### Step 3: Additional Information (if needed)
If anything is unclear, you'll receive a notification requesting more details. This might be:
- A clearer photo of your ID
- A link to your official bio page
- Contact information for your office

### Step 4: Approval
Once verified, you'll receive:
- An email confirmation
- An in‑app notification
- Your profile now displays the official badge

Your dashboard will also update to include official tools.

## Staff Delegation

Many elected officials have staff who help monitor correspondence and prepare for meetings. You can delegate access to your official tools.

### Adding a Delegate

1. From your official dashboard, go to **Settings > Staff Delegation**.
2. Click **"Add Delegate."**
3. Enter the CivicNotes username of your staff member.
4. Choose permissions:
   - **View only** – Can see your dashboard and inbox but cannot take actions.
   - **Respond** – Can acknowledge promises and respond to petitions on your behalf (actions attributed to you).
   - **Full access** – Can do everything except change delegation settings.
5. Send invitation. The staff member must accept.

### Managing Delegates

- You can have multiple delegates.
- Delegates are listed with their permissions and last active date.
- Revoke access at any time.

> **Note:** All actions taken by delegates are logged and attributed to you. Choose delegates carefully.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I be verified if I'm a candidate, not yet elected?
No. Verification is for current officeholders and official staff. Candidates can use regular accounts and should clearly identify themselves as candidates in their profile.

### What if I leave office?
Your official badge will be removed, but your account remains active as a regular user. Past official actions (acknowledgments, responses) remain public.

### Can I have both a personal and official account?
We recommend using one account with the official badge. This keeps your engagement history together. If you prefer separate accounts, contact support for guidance.

### What if my staff member already has a CivicNotes account?
They can be added as a delegate using their existing username. No need for a separate account.

### How long does verification take?
- Email method: Usually 1–2 business days (automated checks may be faster).
- Document method: 3–5 business days.
- In‑person: At the meeting, plus up to 24 hours for system update.
- Reference method: As soon as your reference confirms.

### Can I verify multiple roles (e.g., Council member and Planning Commissioner)?
Yes. If you hold multiple positions, you can indicate all relevant bodies in your profile. You'll receive notifications for all.

### What if someone impersonates an official?
Impersonation is a violation of our Code of Conduct. Report it immediately using the "Report" button on their profile. Verified officials have badges – if you see someone claiming to be official without a badge, report it.

### Do I have to respond to every promise?
No. The Promise Tracker is a tool for transparency, not a mandate. However, responding builds trust and improves your district's Φ‑score. Even a brief acknowledgment ("Thank you, we're looking into this") is valued.

## Tips for New Verified Officials

### First Week

- **Complete your profile** – Add a photo, brief bio, and office contact info (optional).
- **Explore your dashboard** – Click around, see what's in your Promise Inbox, review upcoming meetings.
- **Set notification preferences** – Decide how often you want email digests.
- **Introduce yourself** – Consider posting a brief announcement in your district's feed (visible to constituents).

### First Month

- **Acknowledge at least one promise** – Practice using the tools with a low‑stakes response.
- **Review agenda Φ‑scores** – Before each meeting, check which items have high engagement.
- **Meet your delegates** – If you have staff, ensure they're set up and understand their permissions.
- **Attend a Guild Council meeting** (as observer) – Understand how the platform governs itself.

### Ongoing

- **Weekly check‑in** – 15 minutes to review new promises and upcoming agenda items.
- **Monthly review** – Look at district Φ‑score trends; what's improving, what needs attention?
- **Quarterly engagement** – Consider a "virtual town hall" using CivicNotes' tools.
