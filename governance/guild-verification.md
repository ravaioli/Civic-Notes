# Guild Verification

Guilds are the backbone of collective action on CivicNotes, but with that power comes responsibility. The verification process ensures that every guild claiming to represent a community is legitimate, transparent, and accountable. This guide explains how guilds are created, vetted, and approved—and how they remain accountable over time.

---

## Why Verification Matters

Verification protects the integrity of the platform and the trust of the community. It ensures that:

- **Guilds are real** – They represent actual people with a shared purpose, not astroturf or bad actors.
- **Guilds are transparent** – Their charter, membership, and governance are publicly visible.
- **Guilds are accountable** – They can be challenged, reviewed, and, if necessary, removed.
- **Guilds have legitimacy** – Endorsements and proposals from verified guilds carry weight.

Without verification, anyone could claim to represent a community, diluting the voices of those who do the real work.

---

## Verification Lifecycle Overview
```markdown
Application → Community Comment → Verification Vote → Approval → Ongoing Oversight → (Optional) Re‑verification/Revocation
```

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Application** | Founding members submit charter, member list, and evidence of local grounding. |
| **Community Comment** | 14‑day public comment period for questions and concerns. |
| **Verification Vote** | Existing guilds and high‑reputation users vote on approval. |
| **Approval** | If threshold met, guild is verified and gains access to tools. |
| **Ongoing Oversight** | Guilds must maintain activity and participate in governance. |
| **Re‑verification** | Every two years, guilds undergo a lighter review. |
| **Revocation (if needed)** | Process for removing guild status due to violations or inactivity. |

## Step 1: Application

### Who Can Apply

Any group of at least **three verified CivicNotes users** can apply to form a guild. Founding members should have accounts in good standing (no active suspensions, preferably with some history of constructive participation).

### Required Materials

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Guild Name** | Unique, descriptive, and not misleading. |
| **Short Description** | One‑sentence summary (appears in guild cards). |
| **Charter** | Full document outlining mission, focus areas, membership criteria, governance model, and conflict resolution process. See [Creating a Guild](../guild-guide/creating-guild.md) for template. |
| **Founding Members** | List of at least three verified usernames who consent to be founders. |
| **Focus Areas** | Selection from platform domains (Ecology, Economics, Politics, Spirituality, Culture) and optional topics. |
| **Evidence of Local Grounding** | Links to existing community presence, social media, press mentions, events attended, etc. Helps demonstrate the guild is rooted in real‑world community. |
| **Conflict of Interest Disclosure** | Any funding sources or affiliations with commercial/political entities. |

### Submission

Applications are submitted via the **"Start a New Guild"** form in the Guilds section. Once submitted, the application enters a **draft state** visible only to the applicants until they confirm final submission. After final submission, it becomes public and the clock starts.

---

## Step 2: Community Comment Period

Duration: **14 days**

### Purpose

- Allow the broader CivicNotes community to review the proposed guild.
- Raise questions, concerns, or objections.
- Provide transparency and gather input before verification vote.

### What Happens

- The application (including charter and founding members) is published on a public page.
- Any verified user can leave comments.
- Applicants are encouraged to respond to questions.
- Comments are visible to all; they are not votes but input for voters.

### During This Period

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Answer questions clearly and promptly. | Get defensive or argumentative. |
| Clarify any misunderstandings. | Ignore valid concerns. |
| Update charter if feedback reveals gaps (rare, but possible). | Make substantive changes without noting them. |

### If Significant Concerns Arise

- Commenters may raise issues about the guild's legitimacy, governance, or potential conflicts.
- Applicants should address these transparently.
- If concerns cannot be resolved, they may affect the subsequent vote.

---

## Step 3: Verification Vote

After the comment period closes, the application moves to a **verification vote**.

### Who Votes

The electorate consists of:

| Voter Group | Weight | Rationale |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **All currently verified guilds** | 1 vote per guild | Existing guilds have a stake in maintaining the integrity of the guild system. |
| **Individual users with high Civic Reputation** (top 5% by Civic Seeds) | Votes weighted by reputation score (1–5, based on percentile) | Rewards sustained, constructive engagement. Prevents a small number of high‑reputation users from dominating, but gives them appropriate influence. |

### Vote Threshold

- **Supermajority of 60%** of weighted votes cast is required for approval.
- Abstentions do not count toward the threshold.
- Voting period: **7 days**.

### Voting Process

1. All eligible voters receive a notification with a link to the application.
2. They review the application and comments.
3. They cast a vote: **Approve**, **Reject**, or **Abstain**.
4. Optional comment field allows voters to explain their decision (helpful for applicants if rejected).

### Possible Outcomes

| Outcome | What Happens Next |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Approved** | Guild is verified. Founders receive access to guild dashboard and tools. Guild is listed in directory. |
| **Not Approved** | Application closes. Founders receive feedback (from comments and vote explanations). They may reapply after 30 days, addressing concerns. |
| **Tie / Threshold not met** | Considered not approved; same as above. |

---

## Step 4: Approval and Onboarding

If approved, the guild is officially verified. Founders receive:

- Access to the **Guild Dashboard**
- Ability to invite members, create proposals, endorse items, etc.
- A public guild profile page
- Invitation to send a representative to the **Guild Council**

### First Steps for New Guilds

1. **Customize profile** – Add logo, cover image, complete description.
2. **Set membership rules** – Open, approval required, or invite‑only.
3. **Recruit members** – Invite founding members and reach out to potential recruits.
4. **Create first endorsement or proposal** – Start building momentum.
5. **Attend Guild Council orientation** – Learn about platform governance.

See [Guild Dashboard Overview](../guild-guide/guild-dashboard.md) for detailed guidance.

---

## Step 5: Ongoing Oversight

Verified guilds are not simply approved forever. They must meet ongoing responsibilities.

### Annual Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Active participation** | Guilds that are inactive for 12 months may be flagged for review. |
| **Guild Council participation** | Send a representative to quarterly Guild Council meetings. |
| **Charter review** | Review charter annually; update if needed. |
| **Conflict of Interest disclosure** | Update any changes in funding or affiliations. |

### Transparency Expectations

- **Charter** must remain public and current.
- **Endorsed items** are public.
- **Proposals** are public (once submitted).
- **Member list** may be public (members can choose privacy settings, but aggregate stats are visible).

### Guild Council Participation

Every verified guild is part of the **Guild Council** – a body that helps govern CivicNotes itself. Responsibilities include:

- Electing the Moderation Council.
- Voting on platform‑wide policy changes.
- Mediating disputes between guilds.
- Approving changes to the verification process.

Failure to participate (e.g., no representative at three consecutive meetings) may lead to review and potential revocation.

---

## Step 6: Re‑Verification (Every Two Years)

To ensure guilds remain active and aligned with their stated purpose, every guild undergoes a **light re‑verification** every two years.

### Process

1. Guild receives notification 60 days before re‑verification due date.
2. Guild submits:
   - Brief update on activities over the past two years.
   - Current charter (with any changes noted).
   - Current membership count.
   - Any changes to conflict of interest disclosure.
3. Submission is published for a **30‑day community comment period**.
4. If no significant objections are raised, the guild's status continues.
5. If objections are raised, a **full verification vote** may be triggered (as in Step 3).

### Possible Outcomes

- **Continued** – No issues; guild remains verified.
- **Probation** – Minor concerns; guild given 90 days to address.
- **Revocation** – Significant concerns or inactivity; guild status removed.

---

## Step 7: Revocation of Guild Status

Guild status can be revoked for cause.

### Grounds for Revocation

| Ground | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Violation of Code of Conduct** | Harassment, hate speech, targeted attacks. |
| **Failure to maintain charter** | Governance abandoned, no internal process. |
| **Inactivity** | No posts, endorsements, or member activity for 12+ months. |
| **Misrepresentation** | Guild claims to represent a community it does not. |
| **Capture by external interests** | Undisclosed funding that compromises mission. |
| **Re‑verification failure** | After probation, issues not resolved. |

### Revocation Process

1. **Complaint filed** – Any user or guild can file a complaint with the Moderation Council.
2. **Investigation** – Council reviews evidence, gives guild opportunity to respond.
3. **Recommendation** – Council recommends revocation or dismissal to the Guild Council.
4. **Guild Council vote** – Supermajority (60%) required to revoke.
5. **Decision published** – With rationale.

### Appeals

Guilds may appeal a revocation decision to the **Moderation Council** within 30 days. The Council's decision is final.

## Special Cases

### Mergers and Name Changes

- **Merger** – Two or more guilds may merge. They must submit a joint application with new charter, and both guilds' memberships vote. The merged entity undergoes verification (simplified process).
- **Name change** – Requires approval by guild members (internal vote) and notification to platform (no re‑verification needed unless name is misleading).

### Dissolution

A guild may voluntarily dissolve by:

1. Internal vote (supermajority).
2. Notifying the platform.
3. Transferring any remaining resources (e.g., unspent bounty funds) to the Guild Council for community use.
4. Public announcement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does verification take?
From application submission to decision, approximately 4 weeks (14 days comment + 7 days vote + processing). Complex cases may take longer.

### Can we reapply if rejected?
Yes, after 30 days. Address feedback from comments and votes in your new application.

### Who decides if a comment is "significant" during re‑verification?
During re‑verification, any objection is reviewed by the Moderation Council, which decides whether it warrants a full vote.

### What if our guild is small but active?
Size doesn't matter; engagement does. A guild of 5 highly active members is more valuable than 100 inactive ones.

### Can guilds from outside the platform's jurisdiction apply?
Guilds must focus on issues within the platform's coverage area (e.g., a specific city or region). If you're advocating for issues elsewhere, you may still form a guild, but its influence will be limited to that area.

### How are conflicts of interest defined?
Any funding from commercial entities, political campaigns, or organizations that could compromise the guild's independence. Full disclosure is required; the community assesses whether it's acceptable.

### What if a guild member is also an official?
Officials can be guild members but may not serve as guild leaders if it creates a conflict. Guilds should disclose such members.

| Aspect | Guild's Rights | Guild's Responsibilities |
|--------|----------------|--------------------------|
| **Endorsements** | Endorse notes, proposals, petitions | Endorse thoughtfully, reflecting member consensus |
| **Proposals** | Create and submit proposals | Ensure proposals align with charter and community |
| **Petitions** | Launch and sponsor petitions | Promote constructively, not deceptively |
| **Governance** | Participate in Guild Council, vote on platform decisions | Send representatives, engage in good faith |
| **Transparency** | Charter and endorsements are public | Keep charter updated, disclose conflicts |
| **Accountability** | Can be reviewed, re‑verified, or revoked | Respond to community concerns, maintain activity |
