# Responding to Promises

The Promise Tracker is the heart of accountability on CivicNotes. When community members verify that you've acknowledged their input, that acknowledgment becomes a public, permanent record. How you respond to these promises shapes trust in your leadership and directly impacts your district's Φ‑score. This guide walks you through the entire response process—from receiving a notification to marking a promise as addressed.

---

## Why Responding Matters

| Without Response | With Response |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Constituents feel unheard | Constituents feel acknowledged |
| Promises linger indefinitely | Progress is visible and tracked |
| Distrust accumulates | Trust is built incrementally |
| Your responsiveness Φ‑score stagnates | Your responsiveness Φ‑score improves |
| You miss opportunities to show leadership | You demonstrate commitment to transparency |

Even a brief acknowledgment signals that you're listening. You don't need to have all the answers immediately—you just need to show up.

---

## The Promise Response Lifecycle
```markdown
Promise Verified → You Receive Notification → You Respond → (if action taken) Mark Addressed
↓ ↓
Acknowledgment Evidence Added
↓ ↓
Public Record Public Record
```

| Stage | Your Role |
|-------|-----------|
| **Promise Verified** | Community has confirmed the accuracy of the acknowledgment. It now appears in your inbox. |
| **You Respond** | You publicly acknowledge the promise, with optional comment. |
| **Action Taken** | When the requested action occurs, you (or a community member) mark it addressed with evidence. |

- An **in‑app notification** (bell icon)

## Step 2: Reviewing the Promise

Before responding, take a moment to understand what's being asked.

### What to Look For

When you click **"View Details,"** you'll see:
```markdown

| PROMISE DETAILS |
| |
| Original Note: "5th & Main traffic study" by Alex · Apr 12, 2025 |
| |
| Summary: "Council Member Martinez, during the Apr 15 meeting, |
| said: 'We need to look seriously at traffic calming for 5th and |
| Main. I'll ask staff to include it in the next transportation |
| report.'" |
| |
| Evidence: |
| 🎥 Video (timestamp) · [View] |
| 📄 Minutes excerpt · [View] |
| |
| Verified by: 8 community members (including Safe Streets Guild) |
| |
| Related Items: |
| • Petition (342 signatures) |
| • 12 supportive CivicNotes |
| |
| [Back to Inbox] [Acknowledge] [Mark Addressed] |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Questions to Ask Yourself

- **Did I actually say this?** (The evidence should be clear.)
- **What exactly did I commit to?** (A study? A report? Further discussion?)
- **What's the current status?** (Has any action been taken since?)
- **Who else needs to be involved?** (Staff? Other departments?)

If the evidence seems inaccurate, you can add a comment noting your perspective. However, the community‑verified status remains unless disputed.

## Step 3: Acknowledging a Promise

Acknowledgment is your public confirmation that you've seen the promise and are responding.

### How to Acknowledge

1. From the promise detail page, click **"Acknowledge."**
2. A response form appears:
```markdown
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| ACKNOWLEDGE PROMISE |
| |
| Promise: 5th & Main traffic study |
| |
| Your response (optional but recommended): |
| [Thank you for raising this. I've asked transportation staff |
| to include this intersection in their upcoming safety review. |
| I expect an update within 60 days. ]
| |
| ☑ Also post this response to the related agenda item |
| |
| [Cancel] [Confirm Acknowledgment] |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

3. Add your response (or leave it blank for a simple acknowledgment).
4. Choose whether to also post to the related agenda item (recommended for visibility).
5. Click **"Confirm Acknowledgment."**

### What Happens Next

- The promise status updates to **Acknowledged** (purple).
- Your response is publicly visible on the promise page.
- The original note author and all verifiers receive notifications.
- The district's **Responsiveness Φ‑score** increases.
- The promise moves from "Needs Response" to "Acknowledged" in your inbox.

### Sample Acknowledgment Responses

| Situation | Sample Response |
|-----------|-----------------|
| You need more time | "Thank you for raising this. I've asked staff to look into it and will report back within 60 days." |
| It's already in progress | "Great news – this is already on the Transportation Committee agenda for May 15. I encourage everyone to attend or submit comments." |
| It's not under your jurisdiction | "I appreciate you bringing this forward. However, this falls under the County's jurisdiction. I've forwarded it to Supervisor Chen and encourage you to also reach out to their office." |
| You need clarification | "Before I can respond fully, could you clarify whether you're requesting a full traffic study or just a speed survey? Staff has asked for more specifics." |
| Simple acknowledgment | "Acknowledged. Thank you for your advocacy on this issue." |

## Step 4: Marking a Promise as Addressed

When the requested action is actually taken, you (or a community member) can mark the promise as **Addressed**.

### What Counts as Addressed?

| Action Type | Examples of "Addressed" |
|-------------|-------------------------|
| **Study completed** | Traffic study published, report released |
| **Policy changed** | Ordinance passed, resolution adopted |
| **Funding allocated** | Budget includes requested amount |
| **Project completed** | Crosswalk painted, signs installed |
| **Process initiated** | Formal review begun, committee formed |

### How to Mark Addressed

1. From the promise detail page, click **"Mark Addressed."**
2. Provide evidence:
```markdown
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| MARK PROMISE AS ADDRESSED |
| |
| Promise: 5th & Main traffic study |
| |
| Evidence of action: |
| ○ Link to resolution/report: [_____________________________] |
| ○ Upload document: [Choose File] |
| ○ Photo: [Choose File] |
| ○ Text description: [_____________________________________] |
| |
| Notes (optional): |
| [The Transportation Committee approved the study at its May 15 |
| meeting. Staff will begin data collection in June.] |
| |
| [Cancel] [Submit for Verification] |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

3. Submit. The promise now enters a **short verification period** (usually 3 days) for community confirmation.

### What Happens Next

- Community members review your evidence.
- If verified, status becomes **Addressed** (green).
- All involved users receive celebration notifications.
- Significant Φ‑score boost for your district.
- The promise appears in "Success Stories" feeds.

> **Note:** If a community member marks it Addressed first, you'll still receive credit. The system tracks who proposed the addressed status.

## Step 5: Handling Disputed Promises

Sometimes promises are disputed—either during initial verification or after acknowledgment.

### If a Promise You Acknowledged Becomes Disputed

1. You'll receive a notification: "Promise #123 has been disputed."
2. The promise status changes to **Disputed** (red).
3. Moderators review the evidence and any comments.
4. You may be asked to provide additional information.
5. Moderators will either:
   - **Uphold** – Promise returns to Verified/Acknowledged status.
   - **Overrule** – Promise is marked invalid (Rejected).

### Your Role

- Cooperate with moderators if they request information.
- You can add a comment explaining your perspective.
- Final decision rests with the Moderation Council.

Disputes are rare but important for maintaining the system's integrity.

## Best Practices for Responding

### Do's

- **Respond promptly** – Even a quick acknowledgment within a week builds trust.
- **Be specific** – Vague responses ("We'll look into it") are less satisfying than concrete next steps.
- **Set expectations** – If it will take time, say so and provide a timeline.
- **Follow through** – If you promise an update by a certain date, deliver it.
- **Credit constituents** – "Thanks to the Safe Streets Guild for raising this" builds goodwill.

### Don'ts

- **Don't ignore promises** – Unacknowledged promises hurt your responsiveness score and erode trust.
- **Don't make promises you can't keep** – It's better to say "I'll investigate" than to promise action you can't deliver.
- **Don't get defensive** – Even if you disagree, respond respectfully.
- **Don't use the platform for campaigning** – Keep responses focused on the issue, not your reelection.

## Response Templates

Feel free to adapt these templates to your style.

### Acknowledgment (Need Time)

> Thank you for bringing this to my attention. I've asked [staff/the relevant department] to look into this and provide more information. I expect to have an update within [X days/weeks]. I appreciate your patience and your advocacy on this issue.

### Acknowledgment (Already in Motion)

> Great news – this is already on the [Committee Name] agenda for [Date]. I encourage you to attend or submit public comments. You can find the agenda here: [link]. Thank you for your engagement.

### Acknowledgment (Out of Jurisdiction)

> I appreciate you raising this, though this issue falls under [County/State/School District] jurisdiction. I've forwarded it to [Official's Name] and encourage you to also reach out to their office. You can contact them at [contact info].

### Acknowledgment (Needs Clarification)

> Before I can respond fully, could you help clarify whether you're requesting [Option A] or [Option B]? Staff has asked for more specifics to ensure we're addressing the right concern. Thank you for your help.

### Marking Addressed

> I'm pleased to share that [action taken, e.g., "the City Council has approved funding for the traffic study"]. You can view the resolution here: [link]. Thank you to everyone who advocated for this – your voices made a difference.

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Multiple Officials Mentioned

If a promise mentions several officials (e.g., the whole Council), anyone can respond. Best practice:
- Coordinate with colleagues to avoid duplicate responses.
- One acknowledgment on behalf of the body is sufficient.
- Use "Council President Martinez on behalf of the City Council" if authorized.

### Scenario 2: Staff Handles Response

If you've delegated response authority to staff:
- Their acknowledgment appears under your name.
- Ensure they understand your preferences for tone and content.
- Review the "Delegate Actions" log in your dashboard to stay informed.

### Scenario 3: Promise References a Past Term

If a promise mentions something from before your time in office:
- Acknowledge it anyway – constituents appreciate knowing you're aware.
- You might say: "I wasn't in office when this was first raised, but I've reviewed the history and am committed to following up."

### Scenario 4: Promise Is Duplicative

If the same issue generates multiple promises:
- Acknowledge each one individually (different constituents may have raised them).
- Consider creating a single tracking item in your own system, but respond to each person.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How quickly should I respond?
Within 7–14 days is ideal. Faster is better, but accuracy matters more than speed.

### What if I have no update to share?
Acknowledge anyway: "Thank you for your patience. I'm still waiting for information from staff and will update you as soon as I have it."

### Can I delete a response?
No, responses are permanent public records. You can add a follow‑up comment if you need to clarify or correct something.

### What if I accidentally acknowledge the wrong promise?
Add a follow‑up comment explaining the error. If it's a serious issue, contact support.

### Do I have to respond to every promise?
You're not legally required to, but your responsiveness score will suffer if you ignore them. Even a brief acknowledgment is better than silence.

### Can my staff respond for me?
Yes, if you've delegated response permissions. Their actions appear under your name, so choose delegates carefully.

### How do I know if a promise is addressed?
The system will notify you when someone (including you) proposes "Addressed" status. You can also check your dashboard for addressed promises.

### What if I disagree with a promise's accuracy?
Add a comment noting your perspective. The community‑verified status remains, but your comment becomes part of the public record.
