# Official Dashboard Overview

Welcome to your official dashboard. This is your command center for understanding community sentiment, tracking promises, and engaging with constituents. Designed specifically for verified government officials, the dashboard brings together everything you need to participate meaningfully on CivicNotes.

This guide walks you through every section of the dashboard and explains how to use each tool effectively.

---

## Accessing Your Dashboard

1. Log in to CivicNotes with your verified official account.
2. You'll automatically land on your personalized dashboard.
3. If you ever need to return, click **"Dashboard"** in the top navigation bar.

Your dashboard is divided into several key sections, which we'll explore below.

## Section 1: Upcoming Meetings

This section shows all public meetings scheduled in the next 30 days, prioritized by date.

### What You'll See

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Meeting name** | e.g., "City Council," "School Board" |
| **Date and time** | When the meeting occurs |
| **Item count** | Number of agenda items |
| **Φ‑Score** | Aggregate civic health for all items in this meeting |
| **Note count** | Total CivicNotes across all items |
| **Petition count** | Active petitions targeting this meeting |

### Actions

| Button | What It Does |
|--------|--------------|
| **Prepare** | Opens a special "prep view" with sentiment summaries, top notes, and staff briefings (if any). |
| **View Agenda** | Takes you to the full agenda list for that meeting. |

### Staff Briefings (if enabled)

Some bodies allow staff to add internal notes visible only to officials. These might include:
- Staff recommendations
- Fiscal impact summaries
- Legal considerations

If available, these appear when you click **"Prepare."**

## Section 2: Promise Inbox

The Promise Inbox is where you'll see all verified promises that mention you or your body. This is your accountability hub.

### Promise Statuses You'll See

| Status | Meaning | Your Action |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Verified** | Community‑confirmed as accurate; awaiting official response. | Acknowledge or address. |
| **Acknowledged** | You (or another official) have responded. | (No action needed unless following up.) |
| **Addressed** | Action has been taken. | (Celebrate – no action needed.) |
| **Disputed** | Under moderator review. | (Wait for resolution.) |

### For Each Promise, You Can

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Acknowledge** | Publicly confirm you've seen the promise. Add an optional comment (e.g., "Thank you, we're reviewing this."). |
| **Mark Addressed** | Indicate that action has been taken. Requires evidence (link, document, photo). |
| **Later** | Snooze for follow‑up (removes from main view but keeps in your "Pending" list). |
| **View Details** | See full promise information: original note, evidence, verification history, and any previous responses. |

### Acknowledgment Tips

- Even a brief acknowledgment is valued by constituents.
- If you need more time, say so: "We're gathering information and will report back within 60 days."
- All responses are public – write with that in mind.

See [Responding to Promises](responding-to-promises.md) for detailed guidance.

## Section 3: Your District Φ‑Score

This section shows the civic health score for your geographic area (if you're district‑based) or your body's jurisdiction.

### Φ‑Score Breakdown

| Sub‑metric | What It Measures | Your Role |
|------------|------------------|-----------|
| **Participation** | Volume of CivicNotes from your district | Encourage constituents to participate. |
| **Clarity** | Coherence of sentiment (not noise) | Clear questions help you understand issues. |
| **Responsiveness** | How often officials acknowledge/act | This is directly affected by your engagement. |
| **Connections** | Network density among constituents | Build community connections. |

### Actions

- **View Details** – See trends over time, compare with other districts, and identify which topics are driving changes.
- **Compare with Other Districts** – Understand how your area's civic health relates to others (anonymized).

Your responsiveness score is directly influenced by how you engage with the Promise Inbox. Each acknowledgment and addressed promise boosts this metric.

## Customizing Your Dashboard

- **Meeting reminders** – 24 hours, 1 hour, or at start.

## Best Practices for Using Your Dashboard

### Daily (5–10 minutes)

- Check **Promise Inbox** for new items needing response.
- Scan **Upcoming Meetings** for tomorrow's agenda.
- Review **Notifications** for urgent items.

### Weekly (15–20 minutes)

- Respond to any pending promises.
- Review **District Φ‑Score** trends.
- Look at **Top Constituent Concerns** to understand emerging issues.
- Prepare for next week's meetings using the analytics.

### Monthly (30 minutes)

- Download and review the **Full Report**.
- Discuss trends with staff or colleagues.
- Adjust your engagement approach based on data.

### Quarterly

- Attend **Guild Council** meeting (as observer or participant).
- Review your **Responsiveness Metrics** compared to peers.
- Set goals for the next quarter (e.g., "Improve response time to under 10 days").

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I see notes from my district only?
Yes. In any agenda view, use the **"Neighbor Filter"** to see notes from your district first. The dashboard's constituent concerns are already district‑filtered.

### What if I accidentally acknowledge the wrong promise?
You can add a follow‑up comment clarifying. If it's a serious error, contact support.

### How are response times calculated?
From the date a promise becomes **Verified** to the date you first **Acknowledge** it (or **Address** it directly). Only your first response counts.

### Can my staff respond for me?
Yes, if you've given them **Respond** or **Full access** permissions. Their responses appear under your name, so choose delegates carefully.

### What if I disagree with a promise's accuracy?
You can add a comment noting your perspective, but the community‑verified status remains. If you believe it's fundamentally false, you can flag it for moderator review.

### How do I get help with the dashboard?
- Use the **"Help"** button in the bottom right for context‑sensitive tips.
- Email **officialsupport@civicnotes.org** for technical issues.
- Join the monthly **Officials User Group** call (see your email for invitations).
