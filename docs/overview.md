# CivicNotes Overview

Welcome to **CivicNotes** – a social platform for regenerative civic engagement. CivicNotes transforms how communities interact with local government by making public meetings, agendas, and decisions visible, accountable, and actionable. It is a space where residents, organizations, and officials can co‑create a healthier, more participatory democracy.

This document provides a high‑level introduction to the platform’s purpose, core concepts, and the ecosystem it supports.

---

## Vision

CivicNotes envisions a world where every resident has the tools to understand, influence, and track the decisions that shape their community. By weaving together transparent data, collective intelligence, and accountable feedback loops, we aim to make local governance a living, responsive part of everyday life—a true **Visible Heart** for the city.

---

## Core Concepts

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Φ‑Score** | A composite metric (0–100) that measures the civic health of an agenda item, district, or topic. It combines participation volume, sentiment coherence, official responsiveness, and community connectivity. The Φ‑Score makes the state of local democracy legible at a glance. |
| **CivicNote** | A short public comment on a specific agenda item. Users can tag their note with an **intent**: Support, Oppose, Question, Proposal, or Witness. Notes are the basic building blocks of conversation. |
| **Promise Tracker** | A community‑verified system that tracks when officials acknowledge or act on citizen input. Anyone can propose a “promise” by linking evidence (video timestamps, minutes, etc.) to a note; the community then verifies it. This creates a public, tamper‑resistant record of accountability. |
| **Guilds** | Verified, self‑organizing collectives of users who share a common civic purpose (e.g., Safe Streets Guild, Housing Justice Guild). Guilds can endorse notes, sponsor proposals, and participate in platform governance. |
| **Petitions** | Formal expressions of community will, often derived from regenerative actions. Petitions collect verified signatures and can automatically create Promise Tracker records when they reach their goal. |
| **Regenerative Actions** | A library of pre‑defined, strategically aligned interventions drawn from the Solarpunk Mandala. Users can browse by domain (Ecology, Economics, Politics, etc.), adapt them to local context, and turn them into proposals or petitions. |

## How CivicNotes Works – A Quick Tour

### 1. Discover
- Browse upcoming public meetings (synced from Legistar or similar systems).
- See each agenda item with its **Φ‑Score**, sentiment summary, and a preview of recent notes.
- Filter by neighborhood, topic, or guild to find what matters to you.

### 2. Contribute
- Leave a **CivicNote** on any agenda item. Tag your intent so others understand your perspective.
- If you spot an official acknowledgment of your note (during a meeting or in documents), propose a **Promise** with evidence.

### 3. Collaborate
- Join or form a **Guild** with neighbours who share your concerns.
- Suggest that your guild endorse a note or proposal to amplify its visibility.
- Work together on a **Proposal** using the Regenerative Action Library.

### 4. Track
- Watch the **Promise Tracker** to see which officials respond to citizen input.
- Verify promises made by others – every verified promise increases the community’s Φ‑Score.
- Celebrate when a promise is addressed; your dashboard shows your personal impact.

### 5. Escalate
- If an issue needs broader support, launch a **Petition** from any note or proposal.
- Gather verified signatures from neighbours. When the goal is met, the petition automatically creates a Promise Tracker record targeting the relevant body.

## Who CivicNotes Is For

| Role | How They Use CivicNotes |
|------|-------------------------|
| **Residents** | Stay informed, voice concerns, track official responses, connect with neighbours, and build collective power. |
| **Guilds & Community Organizations** | Coordinate advocacy, endorse community‑supported ideas, track impact, and participate in platform governance. |
| **Elected Officials & Staff** | Understand constituent priorities in real time, respond to promises, and build trust through transparent engagement. (Officials have a dedicated dashboard with aggregated views and response tools.) |
| **Moderators & Stewards** | Community‑elected users who help verify disputed promises, enforce the code of conduct, and ensure the integrity of the platform’s governance. |

---

## Guiding Principles

- **Visible Heart:** Make the health of civic life visible, so it can be nurtured collectively.
- **Regenerative, Not Extractive:** Every interaction should increase the capacity for future collaboration and trust.
- **Dual Power:** Build parallel institutions and accountability structures that can coexist with, and eventually transform, legacy systems.
- **Consciousness‑First:** Honour the inner dimensions of civic life – hope, grief, solidarity – alongside external metrics.
- **Community Governance:** Power over the platform is distributed through guilds, councils, and transparent processes.
