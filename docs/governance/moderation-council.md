# Moderation Council

The Moderation Council is one of two primary governing bodies on CivicNotes (alongside the Guild Council). It serves as the platform's independent judiciary—responsible for resolving disputes, enforcing the Code of Conduct, and protecting the integrity of community processes. This guide explains the Council's role, composition, powers, and procedures.

---

## Why a Moderation Council?

In a platform built on trust and transparency, disagreements are inevitable. The Moderation Council exists to:

- **Resolve disputes** fairly and consistently when community processes fail.
- **Enforce community standards** by reviewing Code of Conduct violations.
- **Protect integrity** by ensuring verification processes (promises, guilds) are not abused.
- **Provide recourse** for users who believe they've been treated unfairly.
- **Reduce burden** on the broader community by handling complex cases.

The Council acts as a check and balance—independent from the Guild Council and platform administration—ensuring that power is distributed and accountable.

---

## Council Composition

### Size

The Moderation Council consists of **7 members**.

### Eligibility

To serve on the Moderation Council, a user must:

- Have a verified CivicNotes account (minimum 1 year old).
- Have a clean record (no active Code of Conduct violations in the past 2 years).
- Not currently serve on the Guild Council (separation of powers).
- Be nominated by a guild or by petition (100 verified signatures from distinct users).

### Term Length

- **2 years**, staggered.
- Approximately half the seats are up for election each year.
- Members may serve a maximum of **2 consecutive terms** (4 years total), after which they must take at least one term off.

### Compensation

Currently, the Moderation Council is a volunteer position. Future Φ‑economy features may include token rewards for service.

## Election Process

### Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Nomination** | 30 days | Candidates are nominated by guilds or via petition. |
| **Vetting** | 14 days | Nominations reviewed for eligibility. |
| **Campaign** | 14 days | Candidates may post statements (designated space). |
| **Voting** | 14 days | Guild Council votes to elect. |
| **Onboarding** | 7 days | New members trained and seated. |

### Who Votes?

The **Guild Council** elects Moderation Council members. Each guild gets one vote.

**Rationale:** Guilds represent organized community voices. Entrusting them with electing the moderation body creates accountability while maintaining independence from direct popular vote (which could be swayed by factions).

### Voting Method

- **Ranked choice voting** (single transferable vote).
- Results announced publicly.
- New members are seated immediately after onboarding.

### Vacancies

If a member resigns or is removed mid‑term:
- The seat remains vacant until the next regular election unless it's within 6 months of the election, in which case it remains unfilled.
- For vacancies early in a term, a special election may be called by the Guild Council.

## Council Structure

### Officers

The Council elects from among its members:

| Role | Responsibilities | Term |
|------|------------------|------|
| **Chair** | Presides over meetings, manages agenda, represents Council publicly. | 1 year (renewable) |
| **Vice Chair** | Assists Chair, presides in their absence. | 1 year (renewable) |
| **Clerk** | Maintains records, tracks cases, manages schedules. | 1 year (renewable) |

Officers are elected by simple majority of the Council.

### Meetings

- **Regular meetings:** Bi‑weekly (virtual).
- **Special meetings:** As needed for urgent cases.
- **Quorum:** 4 members (majority) required for decisions.
- **Decisions:** Simple majority unless otherwise specified.
- **Observers:** Guild Council may send a non‑voting observer; meetings are otherwise closed for case confidentiality.

## Jurisdiction and Powers

### What the Council Handles

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Promise disputes** | When a promise is disputed during verification, or after verification. |
| **Guild disputes** | Internal guild conflicts that cannot be resolved, or disputes between guilds. |
| **Code of Conduct violations** | Harassment, impersonation, fraud, threats, etc. |
| **Verification appeals** | Appeals of guild verification decisions, or individual verification denials. |
| **Platform administration appeals** | Appeals of actions taken by platform administrators (e.g., account suspension). |
| **Election disputes** | Challenges to Guild Council or Moderation Council election results. |

### What the Council Does NOT Handle

- **Policy decisions** – These belong to the Guild Council.
- **Feature requests** – These go to platform developers.
- **Day‑to‑day user disputes** – These should first go through community processes (e.g., guild internal resolution).
- **Criminal matters** – These are referred to appropriate authorities.

### Powers

The Council may:

- **Dismiss** a case with explanation.
- **Facilitate mediation** between parties.
- **Issue rulings** on disputed promises (uphold, overrule, or modify).
- **Impose sanctions** for Code of Conduct violations (warnings, temporary suspensions, permanent bans).
- **Recommend revocation** of guild status to the Guild Council (which votes on final decision).
- **Interpret** the Code of Conduct in specific cases.

## Case Process

### Step 1: Filing a Case

Any user can file a case using the **"Request Moderation"** form, available:

- On any disputed promise page.
- On any user profile (for Code of Conduct complaints).
- Via a dedicated link in the Help section.

The form requires:
- **Parties involved** (usernames).
- **Description** of the issue.
- **Evidence** (links, screenshots, etc.).
- **Prior resolution attempts** (what have you tried already?).

### Step 2: Initial Review

A Council member (rotating duty) reviews within **72 hours** to determine:

- Is this within our jurisdiction?
- Has the user attempted reasonable prior resolution?
- Is there sufficient information to proceed?

If the case is accepted, it enters the docket. If rejected, the filer receives an explanation and may appeal to the full Council.

### Step 3: Assignment

Cases are assigned to a **primary reviewer** (one Council member) who:

- Gathers additional information if needed.
- Contacts all parties.
- Attempts to facilitate resolution.

### Step 4: Investigation

The primary reviewer:

- Reviews all evidence.
- May request additional information from parties.
- May consult with other Council members.
- May hold a virtual hearing (rare, for complex cases).

Timeline: **14–30 days**, depending on complexity.

### Step 5: Deliberation

The primary reviewer presents findings to the full Council. Council discusses and votes.

### Step 6: Decision

Decision includes:
- **Findings of fact**.
- **Interpretation** of relevant rules.
- **Ruling** (e.g., promise upheld, sanction imposed).
- **Rationale**.

Decisions are published (with personal information redacted) in a public **Case Log**.

### Step 7: Appeals

Decisions may be appealed to the **full Council** (if decided by a panel) or, for major cases, to the **Guild Council**. Appeals must be filed within **14 days** and must demonstrate:

- Procedural error.
- New evidence not previously available.
- Misinterpretation of rules.

The appellate body may uphold, reverse, or remand for reconsideration.

## Types of Cases

### Promise Disputes

When a promise is disputed (during verification or after), the Council determines:

- Is the evidence sufficient?
- Does the evidence accurately reflect official acknowledgment?
- Were there procedural errors in verification?

**Possible rulings:**
- **Uphold** – Promise remains verified.
- **Overrule** – Promise is invalid (status changed to Rejected).
- **Remand** – Send back for re‑verification with guidance.

### Guild Disputes

**Internal guild disputes** (e.g., leadership conflicts, governance breakdown):

- Council encourages internal resolution first.
- If unable, Council may facilitate mediation.
- As last resort, Council may recommend revocation to Guild Council.

**Inter‑guild disputes** (e.g., disagreements over endorsements, territory):

- Council facilitates resolution.
- May issue non‑binding recommendations.
- If unresolved, may refer to Guild Council for policy decisions.

### Code of Conduct Violations

The Code of Conduct prohibits:

- Harassment, threats, or intimidation.
- Impersonation.
- Fraud (e.g., fake signatures, false evidence).
- Hate speech.
- Spam or disruption.
- Privacy violations.

**Sanctions (graduated):**

| Sanction | Description | Duration |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Warning** | Formal notice; no loss of access. | Permanent record |
| **Temporary suspension** | Cannot post, sign, or vote. | 7–30 days |
| **Extended suspension** | Longer restriction. | 31–180 days |
| **Permanent ban** | Indefinite loss of access. | Permanent |
| **Feature restrictions** | E.g., cannot propose promises. | Varies |

Sanctions are public (anonymized in case log) except warnings, which may be private.

### Verification Appeals

**Guild verification denials** may be appealed to the Council. The Council reviews:

- Was the process followed correctly?
- Were there errors in evaluating the application?
- Is new information available?

Council may uphold the denial, overturn it, or recommend reconsideration.

**Individual verification denials** (e.g., official verification) follow a similar process.

### Platform Administration Appeals

If a user believes platform administrators (staff) acted improperly (e.g., unjust account suspension), they may appeal to the Council. The Council reviews for:

- Adherence to policies.
- Procedural fairness.
- Proportionality.

Council may uphold, overturn, or modify the admin action.

## Transparency and Accountability

### Public Case Log

All final decisions are published in a **Case Log**, including:

- Case number and type.
- Summary of issue.
- Decision and rationale.
- Sanction (if any).
- Date.

Personal information (usernames, identifying details) is redacted.

### Annual Report

Each year, the Council publishes a report including:

- Number and types of cases handled.
- Trends observed.
- Recommendations for policy or process improvements.
- Council member attendance and participation.

### Council Review

Council members are subject to review by the Guild Council:

- Annual confidence vote (simple majority).
- Members may be removed for cause (e.g., neglect of duty, Code violations) by 2/3 vote of Guild Council.

## Relationship with Guild Council

| Aspect | Moderation Council | Guild Council |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Role** | Judicial | Legislative |
| **Membership** | 7 elected individuals | Representatives from each guild |
| **Selection** | Elected by Guild Council | Guilds send representatives |
| **Decisions** | Case‑specific rulings | Policy, strategy, resource allocation |
| **Accountability** | To Guild Council (annual review) | To member guilds |

The two councils are designed to balance each other—the Guild Council sets the rules, the Moderation Council interprets and enforces them.

## Becoming a Council Member

### Eligibility Checklist

- [ ] Verified account for at least 1 year.
- [ ] No active Code of Conduct violations in past 2 years.
- [ ] Not currently on Guild Council.
- [ ] Nominated by a guild OR have 100 verified signatures.
- [ ] Willing to commit ~5–10 hours per month.

### How to Get Nominated

- **Guild nomination** – A guild may nominate you (requires guild vote). Contact guilds whose missions align with your interests.
- **Petition nomination** – Gather 100 verified signatures from distinct users. Use the petition tool to launch a "Council Nomination" petition.

### Campaigning

During the campaign period, you may:

- Post a candidate statement (designated space).
- Participate in candidate forums (virtual).
- Answer questions from the community.

Campaigning outside designated spaces (e.g., mass messaging) is prohibited.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I file a case?
Use the **"Request Moderation"** link in the Help section or on any disputed promise page.

### How long do cases take?
Most cases are resolved within 30 days. Complex cases may take longer.

### Can I appeal a decision?
Yes, within 14 days. See appeals process above.

### Are Council meetings public?
Deliberations are confidential to protect parties. Final decisions are public.

### Can I talk to a Council member about my case?
During an active case, communicate only through the designated process (via the primary reviewer). Ex parte communication is prohibited.

### What if a Council member has a conflict of interest?
They must recuse themselves. The case will be assigned to another member.

### How are Council members removed?
By 2/3 vote of the Guild Council, following a recommendation from the Council itself (for internal issues) or a complaint process.

### Does the Council have any enforcement power?
Sanctions are enforced by the platform (e.g., suspensions are implemented by administrators). The Council's role is to decide; administrators implement.
