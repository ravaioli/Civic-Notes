# Glossary of Terms

This glossary defines key terms used throughout CivicNotes. Whether you're new to the platform or a seasoned user, this reference will help you understand the language of regenerative civic engagement.

---

## A

### Acknowledged (Promise Status)
A promise status indicating that an official has publicly responded to a verified promise. The response may be supportive, clarifying, or committing to future action. See also: [Promise Tracker](tracking-promises.md).

### Addressed (Promise Status)
A promise status indicating that the requested action has been taken and verified by the community. This is the final, successful stage of a promise's lifecycle. See also: [Promise Tracker](tracking-promises.md).

### Agenda Item
A specific topic or proposal listed on a public meeting agenda. Agenda items are the primary unit of organization in CivicNotes—each item has its own page, Φ‑Score, and feed of CivicNotes. See also: [Finding Agenda Items](finding-agenda.md).

### Agenda Item Page
A dedicated page for each agenda item showing the official description, Φ‑Score, sentiment summary, community notes, related petitions, and Promise Tracker records.

### AI Matching
A future feature that would automatically scan meeting transcripts to suggest potential promises by matching official statements to relevant CivicNotes.

### Arena
In the Solarpunk Mandala framework, an arena is a bounded context for regenerative work (e.g., a watershed, a neighborhood, a policy domain). CivicNotes treats each city or district as an arena.

---

## B

### Badge
A visual indicator on user profiles or content showing achievements, roles, or affiliations (e.g., "Verified Resident," "Guild Leader," "Promise Verifier").

### Boundary Permeability Index (BPI)
A metric from the Solarpunk Mandala that measures how open or rigid social boundaries are. In CivicNotes, the "Responsiveness" sub‑metric of the Φ‑Score is a form of BPI, tracking how easily citizen input crosses into official discourse.

### Bounty
A community-funded micro-project, often proposed by guilds, that pays in Φ‑tokens for regenerative work. Bounties are a future feature tied to the Φ‑economy.

---

## C

### Circulation Check
Part of a Regenerative Action's metadata, explaining why the action is regenerative rather than extractive—why power flows to the community rather than being concentrated.

### Civic Seed
A reputation point earned by contributing to CivicNotes—leaving notes, verifying promises, signing petitions, etc. Civic Seeds appear on user profiles and contribute to trust weighting in governance votes.

### CivicNote
A short public comment on a specific agenda item. Notes include an intent tag (Support, Oppose, Question, Proposal, or Witness) and form the basis of conversation on the platform. See also: [Leaving Notes](leaving-notes.md).

### Code of Conduct
The set of community standards governing behavior on CivicNotes. All users agree to follow it upon sign-up.

### Community Verification
The process by which proposed promises are reviewed and voted on by community members. Verification ensures accuracy and builds trust in the Promise Tracker. See also: [Tracking Promises](tracking-promises.md).

### Connections (Φ‑Submetric)
One of the four components of the Φ‑Score, measuring the density of relationships between users—replies, guild endorsements, cross‑neighborhood engagement. Weight: 15%.

### Core Concepts
The fundamental ideas underpinning CivicNotes: Φ‑Score, CivicNotes, Promise Tracker, Guilds, Petitions, and Regenerative Actions. See also: [Core Concepts](core-concepts.md).

---

## D

### Dashboard
The personalized landing page for each user, showing upcoming meetings, recent activity, relevant petitions, and personal metrics. Different dashboards exist for regular users, guild members, and officials.

### Demurrage
A concept from Appendix T where currency has a carrying cost to discourage hoarding. In CivicNotes, this might apply to future Φ‑tokens but not to reputation or Civic Seeds.

### Disputed (Promise Status)
A promise status indicating that community members have challenged its accuracy. Disputed promises are reviewed by moderators. See also: [Tracking Promises](tracking-promises.md).

### Domain
One of the five high‑level categories in the Regenerative Action Library: Ecology, Economics, Politics, Spirituality, and Culture. Each action is tagged with a domain.

### Dual Power
A strategy of building alternative institutions that parallel and eventually supplant existing power structures. CivicNotes is a dual‑power tool—it creates citizen‑controlled accountability infrastructure alongside official systems.

---

## E

### Endorsement
When a guild officially supports a CivicNote, proposal, or petition. Endorsed items receive a guild badge and increased visibility. See also: [Guild Endorsements](../guild-guide/endorsements.md).

### Evidence
Material (video timestamp, document excerpt, screenshot) submitted to support a promise proposal or verification. Evidence is the foundation of the Promise Tracker's credibility.

### Expired (Petition Status)
A petition status indicating that the deadline passed without reaching the signature goal. Expired petitions can be relaunched.

---

## F

### Filter
A tool for narrowing lists of agenda items, petitions, or actions by criteria such as date, body, neighborhood, Φ‑Score, or intent.

### Footer
The bottom section of every page, containing links to About, Code of Conduct, Privacy Policy, API, and contact information.

---

## G

### Game B
A concept from Appendix R describing cooperative, regenerative systems rather than competitive, extractive ones. CivicNotes aims to be a Game B platform for civic engagement.

### Getting Started
The onboarding process for new users, including account creation, neighborhood verification, and first steps. See also: [Getting Started](getting-started.md).

### Glossary
This document—a reference for all key terms used on the platform.

### Goal (Petition)
The target number of verified signatures a petition aims to collect. Goals are set during petition creation and cannot be changed after launch.

### Government Body
An official entity such as City Council, School Board, Planning Commission, etc. Petitions and promises are targeted at specific bodies.

### Guild
A verified, self-organizing collective of CivicNotes users who share a common civic purpose (e.g., Safe Streets Guild, Housing Justice Guild). Guilds can endorse content, sponsor proposals, and participate in platform governance. See also: [Guild Guide](../guild-guide/creating-guild.md).

### Guild Council
A governing body composed of representatives from each verified guild. The Guild Council helps guide platform decisions, allocate resources, and mediate disputes.

### Guild Dashboard
The command center for guild members and leaders, showing member lists, active proposals, endorsement queue, and impact metrics.

---

## H

### Healing (Pathway)
One of three pathways in the Regenerative Action framework (along with Liberation and Awakening). Healing actions address past harm and restore relationships.

### Heat Map
A geographic visualization showing the density of signatures, notes, or Φ‑scores across a city or district.

---

## I

### Intent Tag
A label attached to each CivicNote indicating the user's purpose: Support (🔵), Oppose (🔴), Question (🟡), Proposal (🟢), or Witness (👁️). Intent tags power sentiment aggregation.

### In‑Person Verification
A signature verification method where users sign at community events by scanning a QR code or using paper sheets. Useful for reaching residents without internet access.

---

## L

### Legistar
A common software platform used by local governments to publish agendas and minutes. CivicNotes syncs with Legistar (and similar systems like Granicus) to display official meeting data.

### Liberation (Pathway)
One of three pathways in the Regenerative Action framework. Liberation actions focus on dismantling oppressive structures and creating freedom.

### Like
A way to show appreciation for a CivicNote. Likes are public and contribute to a note's visibility.

### Living Library
A metaphor for the Regenerative Action Library—a growing, evolving collection of intervention templates that the community can draw from.

---

## M

### Mark as Addressed
The action of proposing that a promise or petition has been fulfilled. Requires evidence and community verification.

### Mesh Governance
A decentralized governance model from Appendix S where power is distributed across interconnected nodes (guilds, councils, individuals) rather than concentrated in a hierarchy.

### Moderator
A community‑elected user who helps resolve disputes, reviews flagged content, and ensures the Code of Conduct is followed. Moderators serve on the Moderation Council.

### Moderation Council
A small body of elected moderators responsible for handling disputes, appeals, and Code of Conduct enforcement. Decisions are public and appealable to the Guild Council.

### Multi‑Step Wizard
A guided form interface used for complex processes like petition creation or action localization, breaking them into manageable steps.

---

## N

### Navigation Bar
The top section of every page, containing links to main sections: Dashboard, Agenda, Actions, Petitions, Guilds, Φ‑Score, and user profile.

### Neighborhood
The geographic area associated with a user after verification. Users see content from their neighborhood first and can filter by neighborhood.

### Neighbor Filter
A default view that shows CivicNotes from users in your neighborhood before showing city‑wide notes. Builds local connection and relevance.

### Note
See [CivicNote](#civicnote).

### Notification
An alert (in‑app, email, or push) informing users of relevant activity—new notes, petition milestones, promises needing verification, etc.

---

## O

### Official
A verified government user (elected official, staff, clerk) with access to a special dashboard for monitoring and responding to civic activity.

### Official Dashboard
A specialized view for verified officials, showing upcoming meetings with Φ‑scores, Promise Tracker inbox, petition alerts, and responsiveness analytics.

### Oppose (Intent)
An intent tag (🔴) used when a CivicNote expresses disagreement with the agenda item or proposal.

### Overview
A high‑level document introducing CivicNotes, its vision, and core concepts. See also: [Overview](../overview.md).

---

## P

### Participation (Φ‑Submetric)
One of the four components of the Φ‑Score, measuring the volume of CivicNotes relative to population. Weight: 30%.

### Pathway
In the Regenerative Action framework, one of three types of change: Liberation, Healing, or Awakening. Each action is associated with a pathway.

### Persona
A fictional user archetype used in UX design to represent different types of platform users (e.g., Alex the resident, Jamal the guild leader, Martinez the official).

### Petition
A formal request, signed by verified community members, asking a government body to take a specific action. Petitions auto‑create Promise Tracker records when they reach their goal. See also: [Petitions](petitions.md).

### Petition Dashboard
A management interface for petition creators, showing progress, recent signers, endorsements, and mobilization tools.

### Φ (Phi)
The Greek letter used as the symbol for CivicNotes' health metric. Pronounced "fee."

### Φ‑Score (Phi Score)
A composite metric (0–100) measuring the civic health of an agenda item, neighborhood, or guild. Combines Participation, Clarity, Responsiveness, and Connections. See also: [Core Concepts](core-concepts.md).

### Φ‑Token
A future complementary currency that would reward regenerative actions and be spendable at local participating businesses. Part of the long‑term vision.

### Profile
A public page for each user showing their display name, neighborhood, Civic Seeds, badges, note history, and guild memberships.

### Promise
A public record linking an official's acknowledgment or action back to a specific CivicNote, proposal, or petition. Promises are the core unit of accountability. See also: [Tracking Promises](tracking-promises.md).

### Promise Tracker
The system for creating, verifying, and tracking promises through their lifecycle. See also: [Tracking Promises](tracking-promises.md).

### Proposal (Intent)
An intent tag (🟢) used when a CivicNote offers an alternative idea or solution, rather than simply supporting or opposing.

### Proposed (Promise Status)
The initial status of a promise after submission, awaiting community verification.

---

## Q

### Question (Intent)
An intent tag (🟡) used when a CivicNote seeks more information or clarification.

### Quest
A future gamification feature where the platform generates collaborative challenges based on real‑time Φ‑data (e.g., "Boost air quality BPI in District 5").

### Quick Actions
Buttons or shortcuts for common tasks, found on dashboards and relevant pages.

---

## R

### Regenerative Action
A pre‑defined, strategically aligned intervention template drawn from the Solarpunk Mandala. Users can browse, adapt, and turn actions into proposals or petitions. See also: [Regenerative Actions](../actions/overview.md).

### Regenerative Action Library
The collection of all available Regenerative Actions, organized by domain, topic, and pathway. A "living library" that grows over time.

### Rejected (Promise Status)
A final status for promises that have been disputed and found invalid by moderators.

### Reply
A threaded response to a specific CivicNote. Replies appear indented below the original note.

### Responsiveness (Φ‑Submetric)
One of the four components of the Φ‑Score, measuring how often officials acknowledge or act on citizen input (via Promise Tracker). Weight: 30%.

---

## S

### Saved Item
An agenda item, petition, or action that a user has bookmarked for later reference. Saved items appear in a dedicated section of the dashboard.

### Sentiment Bar
A visual summary of CivicNotes on an agenda item, showing the proportion of Support, Oppose, Question, and Proposal intents.

### SMS Verification
A signature verification method where users receive a text message with a code to confirm their identity. Fast and secure.

### Solarpunk Mandala
The philosophical and strategic framework underlying CivicNotes. A comprehensive guide to regenerative civilization, referenced throughout this documentation.

### Soulbound Token (SBT)
A future feature for non‑transferable credentials representing verified achievements or roles (e.g., "Watershed Steward").

### Status
The current stage of a promise (Proposed, Verified, Acknowledged, Addressed, Disputed) or petition (Draft, Active, Threshold Met, Submitted, Expired).

### Submitted (Petition Status)
A petition status indicating that the signature goal was met and the petition has been formally delivered to the target body.

### Support (Intent)
An intent tag (🔵) used when a CivicNote expresses agreement with the agenda item or proposal.

---

## T

### Target Body
The government entity (e.g., City Council, School Board) to which a petition or promise is addressed.

### Threshold Met (Petition Status)
A petition status indicating that the signature goal has been reached. The petition will soon be submitted automatically.

### Timeline Visualization
A graphical representation of a promise's lifecycle, showing key dates and status transitions.

### Toast
A temporary, non‑intrusive notification that appears briefly to confirm an action (e.g., "Note posted successfully").

### Topic
A specific category within a domain in the Regenerative Action Library (e.g., under Ecology: Air Quality, Biodiversity, Proximity and Access).

### Trending
Items with unusually high activity or rapid Φ‑score changes, highlighted in feeds.

---

## U

### Universal Basic Provision (UBP)
A concept from Appendix T where essential needs (food, housing, care) are guaranteed in‑kind. A long‑term vision for CivicNotes' economic layer.

### Upcoming Meetings
A view showing all synced public meetings for the next 30 days, with Φ‑scores and note counts.

### User Guide
The collection of documentation for regular platform users, including getting started, core concepts, and feature guides.

### User Journey
The complete flow of actions a user takes to accomplish a goal on the platform, from discovery to impact.

---

## V

### Verification Period
The time window (usually 7 days) during which community members can vote on a proposed promise's accuracy.

### Verified (Promise Status)
A promise status indicating that enough community members have confirmed the evidence is accurate.

### Verified Resident
A user who has proven they live in a specific neighborhood through SMS, email, or in‑person verification. Required for full participation.

### Visible Heart
A design philosophy and literal feature of CivicNotes—making civic health visible through Φ‑Scores, heat maps, and transparent tracking. Inspired by Appendix T.

---

## W

### Watch/Reminder
A feature allowing users to receive notifications before a meeting or when an agenda item is about to be discussed.

### Web of Trust
A future feature where users build reputation through verified relationships and endorsements, visualized as a network graph.

### Witness (Intent)
An intent tag (👁️) used when a CivicNote observes something about the process itself (e.g., "Public comment was cut short") rather than taking a stance on the issue.

---

## X

### XR (Extended Reality)
A future feature for immersive visualization of proposed changes (e.g., AR walkthroughs of development projects).

---

## Y

### Your Dashboard
The personalized landing page for each user. See also: [Getting Started](getting-started.md).

---

## Z

### Zero-Knowledge Proof
A cryptographic method allowing users to prove they are verified residents without revealing personal data. A future privacy feature.

---

## How to Use This Glossary

- Terms are listed alphabetically.
- **Bold** terms have their own entries.
- *Italicized* terms are emphasized concepts.
- "See also" links point to related terms or guides.
- Terms specific to the Solarpunk Mandala framework are noted with (Appendix X) references.

If you encounter a term not listed here, please [suggest an addition](../contributing.md) or ask in the community forum.
