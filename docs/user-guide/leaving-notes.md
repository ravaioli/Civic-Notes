# Leaving CivicNotes

A CivicNote is your voice on the platform—a short public comment on a specific agenda item. Notes are how you share your perspective, ask questions, offer proposals, and witness the process. Every note contributes to the Φ‑Score and helps your community understand what matters to you.

This guide walks you through composing, tagging, and managing your CivicNotes.

---

## What Is a CivicNote?

A CivicNote is a public message attached to a single agenda item. It appears in the item's feed and is visible to everyone—neighbors, guilds, officials, and the public.

Each note includes:
- Your display name and neighborhood
- The date and time
- An **intent tag** (Support, Oppose, Question, Proposal, or Witness)
- Your message
- Interaction buttons (like, reply, share, suggest guild endorsement)
```markdown
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Alex · District 3 · Apr 12, 2025 · [Proposal 💡] |
| |
| This intersection is already dangerous for kids walking to |
| school. Rezoning without a traffic calming plan is reckless. |
| |
| [👍 12] [💬 3] [🔗 2] [Suggest Guild Endorsement] |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

## How to Leave a CivicNote

### Step 1: Find an Agenda Item
Navigate to an agenda item you care about. See [Finding Agenda Items](finding-agenda.md) for help.

### Step 2: Locate the Composer
Scroll down to the **"Leave a CivicNote"** box, usually above or below the existing notes feed.
```markdown
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Leave a CivicNote on Item #7 |
| |
| [What are your thoughts? ] |
| [________________________________________________________] |
| |
| Intent: ○ Support ○ Oppose ○ Question ○ Proposal ○ Witness |
| |
| [Cancel] [Post Note] |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Step 3: Write Your Note
- Be clear and concise. Focus on the specific item.
- Include relevant details—location, personal experience, data, or questions.
- Avoid personal attacks; stick to the issue.

**Example of a good note:**
> "I live two blocks from 5th and Main and walk my kids to school every day. The crosswalk is poorly marked, and drivers often speed. Before approving more density, we need a traffic calming plan. I propose a traffic study and rapid-flashing beacons."

**Example of a less effective note:**
> "This is stupid. The council never listens anyway."

### Step 4: Choose an Intent Tag
Select the tag that best matches your note's purpose.

| Tag | Icon | When to Use |
|-----|------|-------------|
| **Support** | 🔵 | You agree with the proposal or idea. |
| **Oppose** | 🔴 | You disagree. |
| **Question** | 🟡 | You need more information or clarification. |
| **Proposal** | 🟢 | You have an alternative idea or solution. |
| **Witness** | 👁️ | You're noting something about the process, not taking a stance (e.g., "The meeting started late," "Public comment was cut short"). |

Your intent tag helps aggregate sentiment and guides the Promise Tracker.

### Step 5: Post Your Note
Click **"Post Note"**. Your note will appear immediately in the feed. You'll receive a confirmation toast and a small Civic Seeds reward.

## After You Post

### Viewing Your Note
Your note appears in:
- The agenda item's note feed
- Your profile under **"My Notes"**
- Your dashboard's **"Recent Activity"**

### Interacting with Others' Notes
- **Like** (👍) – Show appreciation or agreement.
- **Reply** (💬) – Engage in threaded discussion.
- **Share** (🔗) – Copy a link to the note or share on social media.
- **Suggest Guild Endorsement** – If you're in a guild, you can recommend that your guild officially endorse the note (see [Guild Endorsements](../guild-guide/endorsements.md)).

### Editing or Deleting
- You can edit your note for up to 15 minutes after posting (click the "..." menu on your note).
- After that, notes are locked to maintain thread integrity. If you need a correction, you can add a reply.
- You can delete your own note at any time; it will be removed from public view but may remain in system logs for moderation purposes.

## Tips for Effective CivicNotes

### Be Specific
Vague notes are less likely to be acknowledged. Include details that officials can act on.

❌ "Fix the roads!"
✅ "The pothole on Elm Street between 2nd and 3rd has been there for three months. Please schedule a repair."

### Use the Right Intent Tag
Tagging correctly helps others understand your perspective and powers the sentiment aggregation.

### Reference Data or Documents
If you have a report, news article, or personal observation, mention it. Links are welcome.

### Be Constructive
Even when opposing, suggest alternatives. Proposal‑tagged notes often get more traction.

### Keep It Civil
Personal attacks or inflammatory language may be removed. Focus on the issue, not the person.

### Stay on Topic
Notes should address the specific agenda item. Off‑topic comments may be moved or hidden by moderators.

## CivicNote Etiquette

- **Assume good faith** – Most people are trying to improve the community.
- **Listen and learn** – Read others' notes before posting; you might find answers or allies.
- **Don't spam** – Repeated identical notes on multiple items may be flagged.
- **Respect privacy** – Don't share others' personal information.
- **Report, don't engage** – If you see harassment or misinformation, use the "Report" button instead of arguing.

## What Happens to Your Note?

Your note contributes to:
- The agenda item's **Φ‑Score** (Participation and Clarity sub‑metrics)
- Sentiment aggregation (support/oppose/etc.)
- Potential **promises** – if an official mentions your idea, someone can create a promise linking to your note
- **Guild endorsements** – if a guild finds your note valuable

Over time, your notes build your reputation (Civic Seeds) and show up on your profile as a record of your civic engagement.

## Common Questions

### Can I post anonymously?
CivicNotes uses your display name (which can be a pseudonym) and verified neighborhood. Your real identity is never shown unless you choose to include it in your profile bio.

### Can I delete a note?
Yes, from the note menu. Deleted notes disappear from public view but are logged for moderation.

### What if someone replies to my note?
You'll receive a notification. You can reply back, like the reply, or ignore it. Healthy debate is encouraged.

### How do I know if my note made a difference?
Watch for:
- **Guild endorsements** – your note gets a badge
- **Promise Tracker entries** – someone links a promise to your note
- **Official acknowledgment** – you or another user can propose a promise when an official mentions it
- **Φ‑Score increases** – your participation helps raise the score
